Beef Stroganoff


★★★★★ 1200 Ratings

Description:

Beef Stroganoff is a luxurious and creamy dish that is perfect for a special dinner. Originating from Russia, this dish features tender strips of beef sautéed in a rich sauce made with mushrooms, onions, and a combination of sour cream and mustard for a velvety, savory finish. It’s often served over egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes to soak up the delicious sauce. Perfect for impressing guests or enjoying a cozy evening meal, Beef Stroganoff is the epitome of comfort food with a touch of sophistication.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef tenderloin or sirloin, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced (button or cremini mushrooms work well)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Cooked egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes (for serving)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

  1. Pat the beef strips dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
  2. Season the beef with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Sauté the Beef

  1. In a large skillet, heat 1 tbsp olive oil and 1 tbsp butter over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add the beef in batches to avoid overcrowding. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until browned. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Onions and Garlic

  1. In the same skillet, add 1 finely chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves. Sauté until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.

Step 4: Add the Mushrooms and Cook

  1. Add the 200g sliced mushrooms to the skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms are golden brown and softened, about 5-7 minutes.

Step 5: Make the Sauce

  1. Sprinkle 2 tbsp flour over the mushroom mixture and stir well to combine.
  2. Slowly pour in 1 cup beef broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Bring to a simmer and cook for 3-5 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly.

Step 6: Add the Mustard and Sour Cream

  1. Stir in 1 tbsp Dijon mustard and 1/2 cup sour cream until the sauce becomes smooth and creamy. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Return the Beef to the Skillet

  1. Return the cooked beef to the skillet, along with any juices that have accumulated. Stir well to coat the beef in the sauce and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat through.

Step 8: Serve

  1. Serve the Beef Stroganoff over your choice of cooked egg noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes.
  2. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

Tips for Perfect Beef Stroganoff:

  • For tender beef, avoid overcooking the meat. Sautéing quickly over medium-high heat helps maintain its tenderness.
  • If you don’t have beef tenderloin, you can use sirloin or rib-eye, but make sure to slice the beef thinly against the grain.
  • You can substitute half of the sour cream with heavy cream for a richer sauce.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, continue simmering until it reaches your desired consistency. If it’s too thick, add a splash of beef broth.

Cultural Significance:

Beef Stroganoff has its roots in Russian cuisine, named after the Stroganov family, aristocrats in 19th-century Russia. Originally, it was made with more luxurious ingredients like calf’s liver and served as a delicacy. Over time, the dish evolved into the popular version we know today, made with beef, mushrooms, and a rich, creamy sauce. It became widely known in the United States and Europe in the mid-20th century, where it was embraced as an elegant yet accessible dinner option. Today, Beef Stroganoff is enjoyed in many variations worldwide, but the core principles of tender beef and creamy sauce remain.
