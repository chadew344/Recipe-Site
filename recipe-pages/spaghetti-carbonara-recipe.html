Spaghetti Carbonara


★★★★★ 1800 Ratings

Description:

Spaghetti Carbonara is a quintessential Italian pasta dish that’s simple, yet packed with flavor. It is made with just a few ingredients – spaghetti, guanciale, eggs, and pecorino Romano – to create a creamy, velvety sauce that coats every strand of pasta. The combination of savory guanciale, rich eggs, and tangy cheese creates a dish that is comforting, indulgent, and incredibly satisfying. While it’s a simple recipe, the technique is key to achieving the perfect Carbonara. This dish is a staple in Roman cuisine and loved around the world!

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale (or pancetta), diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • 50g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, finely grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, for pasta water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional, for sautéing guanciale)

Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the 400g spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente, about 9-10 minutes.
  3. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining the pasta.

Step 2: Prepare the Guanciale

  1. While the pasta is cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the 150g guanciale (or pancetta) to the skillet.
  2. If necessary, add 1 tbsp olive oil to help render the fat.
  3. Sauté the guanciale until crispy and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Once done, remove from heat and set aside.

Step 3: Make the Carbonara Sauce

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the 4 eggs, 100g pecorino Romano, and 50g Parmigiano-Reggiano until smooth.
  2. Season the mixture with freshly ground black pepper to taste. The pepper adds a lovely spice that enhances the creaminess of the sauce.

Step 4: Combine Pasta and Guanciale

  1. Once the pasta is cooked and drained, add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the crispy guanciale.
  2. Quickly toss the pasta with the guanciale to coat it in the rendered fat, ensuring the pasta is well coated.

Step 5: Create the Creamy Carbonara Sauce

  1. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta while tossing continuously. The residual heat from the pasta will gently cook the eggs and create a silky, creamy sauce. Be sure to work quickly to prevent scrambling the eggs.
  2. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta cooking water, a tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 6: Final Seasoning and Serve

  1. Taste the carbonara and adjust the seasoning with more freshly ground black pepper, if desired.
  2. Serve the Spaghetti Carbonara immediately, garnished with extra grated Pecorino Romano and a sprinkle of black pepper.

Tips for Perfect Spaghetti Carbonara:

  • Use fresh eggs for the best texture in your sauce.
  • Guanciale is the traditional choice for Carbonara, but pancetta can be used as a substitute if needed.
  • Don’t add the egg mixture too early – the heat from the pasta should gently cook the eggs to form a creamy sauce, not scramble them.
  • Reserve plenty of pasta water to adjust the sauce consistency as needed.
  • Be careful not to overcook the pasta; it should be al dente, as it will continue to cook when mixed with the hot guanciale and eggs.

Why Spaghetti Carbonara is a Classic:

Spaghetti Carbonara is a staple of Roman cuisine, dating back to at least the mid-20th century. Its roots are often debated, but it’s believed to have been invented by Italian charcoal workers in central Italy. The dish is considered a symbol of the Italian tradition of using few, high-quality ingredients to create bold and flavorful meals. With its creamy texture, savory guanciale, and rich flavors, it’s no wonder Spaghetti Carbonara continues to be one of the most popular pasta dishes in the world.

Variations of Carbonara:

While the classic version of Carbonara sticks to the traditional ingredients, many people love experimenting with different variations. Some common tweaks include adding peas for a touch of sweetness, using different cheeses like Parmesan or Asiago, or even adding a hint of garlic to the sauce. While purists may argue, these variations are fun ways to make the dish your own!
