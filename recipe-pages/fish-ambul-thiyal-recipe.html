Sri Lankan Fish Ambul Thiyal


★★★★★ 1200 Ratings

Description:

Sri Lankan Fish Ambul Thiyal is a classic dish that beautifully blends the flavors of tamarind, garlic, curry leaves, and spices to create a savory, tangy curry that pairs perfectly with steamed rice. The fish is cooked until tender in a rich, aromatic sauce made with a unique blend of spices, creating a deep and flavorful curry. This is a staple in Sri Lankan homes and is often served during special occasions or as a comforting, everyday meal.

Ingredients:

  • 500g firm white fish (e.g., tuna, swordfish, or mackerel), cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, sliced
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Fish

  1. Place the fish pieces in a bowl and season with salt, 1 tbsp curry powder, 1/2 tsp black pepper, and 1 tsp turmeric powder. Mix well to coat the fish with the spices.
  2. Let the fish marinate for 15-20 minutes to absorb the flavors.

Step 2: Prepare the Aromatics

  1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and minced ginger. Sauté until the onions turn soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced green chilies, curry leaves, and cinnamon stick, and sauté for another 2 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Tamarind and Spices

  1. Add the tamarind paste to the pan and mix well with the onions and spices.
  2. Add the remaining 1 tbsp curry powder and 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds, and stir everything together.

Step 4: Add the Fish

  1. Gently add the marinated fish pieces into the pan, ensuring the fish is covered in the tamarind and spice mixture.
  2. Pour in enough water to cover the fish, then bring to a simmer. Let the fish cook for 15-20 minutes until the fish is cooked through and tender.

Step 5: Add Coconut Milk

  1. Stir in the 1 cup coconut milk and let the curry simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly.
  2. Check the seasoning and adjust salt as needed.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Remove from heat and serve the Fish Ambul Thiyal with a side of steamed rice.
  2. Garnish with a few extra curry leaves if desired, and enjoy the tangy, flavorful curry.

Tips for Perfect Fish Ambul Thiyal:

  • Use firm, meaty fish like tuna or swordfish, which hold their shape well in the curry.
  • If tamarind paste is not available, you can use tamarind concentrate or fresh tamarind pulp.
  • Adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or fewer green chilies.
  • For extra richness, you can add a bit more coconut milk towards the end of cooking.

Cultural Significance:

Fish Ambul Thiyal is one of the most iconic dishes in Sri Lankan cuisine. Known for its tangy, spicy flavor, it’s often considered a comforting, soul-warming dish in Sri Lankan homes. Traditionally, it is made with fish caught fresh from the sea, reflecting the island's deep connection to the ocean. The use of tamarind and aromatic spices adds complexity to the flavor profile, making it a true Sri Lankan delicacy.
